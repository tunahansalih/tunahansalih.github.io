## Summary
Ph.D. candidate at Virginia Tech (expected Spring 2027), advised by Prof. Pinar Yanardag. My research focuses on understanding and controlling the internal representations of generative models for image and video — spanning controllable generation, long-horizon video models, diffusion and autoregressive generation, diffusion model distillation, representation steering, mechanistic interpretability, and flow matching. My work has appeared at CVPR, ICCV (Highlight), ICML (Oral), and AAAI, with first-author papers on diffusion models, LoRA composition, motion transfer, and representation learning. During my PhD I have interned at Waymo (open-vocabulary perception), Amazon, and Adobe. Before my PhD, I spent several years building production machine learning systems, including diffusion-based image generation serving 5M+ daily inference requests.
